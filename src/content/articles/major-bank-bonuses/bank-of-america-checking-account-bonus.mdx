## Requirement Stack

Each step in the requirement stack has a specific timing window. Missing any one of them voids the bonus.

1. **Open Advantage Plus Checking** -- new customer only (no BofA checking in past 12 months). Credit cards, savings accounts, CDs, and Merrill investment accounts do not disqualify.

2. **Meet the DD threshold within 90 calendar days of account opening** -- cumulative, not per-deposit. The clock starts on the approval date, not the date of the first deposit. For the $300 tier, that means $5,000 total across all qualifying deposits.

3. **Qualifying direct deposits only** -- employer payroll, government benefits (SSA, VA, tax refunds), or confirmed brokerage ACH transfers. No P2P transfers (Venmo, PayPal, Zelle), no self-transfers from other personal accounts. See the Direct Deposit Path section below.

4. **Keep the account open for the full holding period** -- the safe-close date is 5 calendar months from account opening. Closing before the safe-close date risks a full bonus clawback.

5. **Bonus posts within 60 days of meeting the DD threshold.** Community reports show 2 to 4 weeks in practice, but BofA reserves the right to take up to 60 days.

The 90-day window is cumulative, which is more forgiving than banks that require a single qualifying deposit above a specific amount. Multiple smaller paychecks count toward the total.

## Fee Math: What You Will Actually Pay

The Advantage Plus Banking account carries a $12/mo maintenance fee. Four waiver paths exist: (a) $250+ qualifying direct deposit per statement cycle, (b) $1,500+ daily average balance, (c) Preferred Rewards enrollment, or (d) enrolled student under age 24.

**Scenario 1 (most readers):** Payroll DD is set up to meet the bonus requirement. Any direct deposit over $250/mo triggers the fee waiver automatically. Monthly fee drag: $0. Net bonus after fees for the $300 tier: $300.

**Scenario 2 (edge case):** A lump-sum strategy -- one $5,000 transfer from a confirmed-working brokerage source. No recurring DD means some statement cycles may carry the full $12/mo fee. Worst case over the 5-month holding period: $60 in total monthly fee drag. Net bonus after fees: $300 - $60 = $240.

**Scenario 3 (wrong account):** If the application accidentally opens an Advantage Relationship account ($25/mo fee) without maintaining a $10,000+ combined balance across BofA and Merrill accounts, the monthly fee drag over 5 months is $125. Always verify the account type is Advantage Plus before submitting.

The bottom line: set up recurring payroll DD of $250+ per month and the monthly fee drag is zero. BofA also offers SafeBalance Banking at $4.95/mo, waivable for Preferred Rewards members or students under 24, but SafeBalance applicants typically cannot hit the $5,000 or $10,000 thresholds within 90 days on modest income.

## Screening and Eligibility

**ChexSystems:** BofA uses ChexSystems, but 20 community data points show most applicants experience no hard inquiry during standard checking account applications. Applicants with unpaid bank debts or involuntary closures on a ChexSystems report face lower approval odds, but the inquiry itself is a soft pull for most.

**Credit pull:** Soft pull only if overdraft protection is declined at application. Accepting overdraft protection may trigger a hard credit inquiry. Decline it unless overdraft coverage is specifically needed.

**Prior customer rule:** No BofA personal checking account in the past 12 months. BofA credit cards, savings accounts, CDs, and Merrill investment accounts do NOT count against eligibility. Closed checking accounts older than 12 months are eligible.

**State availability:** The online promotional offer is nationwide. In-branch offers vary by market and sometimes carry different -- occasionally higher -- bonus amounts. Calling the local branch before applying online is worth 5 minutes.

**Bonus stacking:** One bonus per customer per promotion. After closing a BofA checking account, the 12-month eligibility clock resets, allowing the bonus to be earned again in a future promotion cycle.
