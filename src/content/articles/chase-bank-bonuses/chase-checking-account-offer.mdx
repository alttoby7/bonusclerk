## Requirement Stack: What You Actually Have to Do

The requirement stack for each Chase bonus has specific timing windows. Missing them does not extend the deadline -- the window closes.

**Total Checking ($300):**
The qualifying direct deposit must post to the account within 90 days of account opening. One deposit of $500 or more is sufficient -- it does not need to be recurring. The bonus amount posts 10-15 business days after the qualifying direct deposit is received. The holding period is 6 months; closing before that date triggers a bonus clawback per Chase's terms.

**Savings ($200, or part of the $900 combo):**
Deposit $15,000 in new money within 20 days of account opening. "New money" means funds that were not held at Chase or any Chase affiliate at the time of account opening. Maintain the full $15,000 balance for 90 consecutive days; partial days below the threshold restart the clock. The capital lockup is $15,000 for approximately 110 days total (20-day deposit window + 90-day hold).

**Business Complete Checking ($300):**
Deposit $2,000 or more within 30 days. Maintain that deposit for 60 days. The $2,000 capital lockup also serves as the fee waiver condition, so there is no fee drag if the balance is maintained. Holding period is 6 months.

**Private Client Checking ($1,000):**
Deposit $150,000 or more in new money within the application window. Maintain the balance for 90 days. Capital lockup is $150,000 for 90 days. Must be completed in-branch with a Private Client banker. Holding period is 6 months. No monthly fee applies regardless of balance activity.

For the $900 combo: open Total Checking and Savings simultaneously. The checking DD and savings deposit requirements run concurrently. The additional $400 bonus requires completing both the checking and savings requirement stacks.

## Fee Math: What You Net After the Monthly Charges

Monthly fee drag is the most frequently omitted element from competitor coverage of bank bonuses. Four accounts, four different fee structures -- here is the full calculation for each.

**Total Checking: $12/month**

Fee waived if any one condition is met:
- $500 or more in qualifying electronic deposits each statement period
- $1,500 or more daily balance
- $5,000 or more average daily balance across linked Chase accounts

If the fee is waived via qualifying direct deposit (the same transaction that earns the bonus), the total monthly fee drag is $0. Net bonus after fees: $300.

If no waiver condition is met: $12 x 6 months = $72 total fee drag. Net bonus after fees: $228.

**Savings: $5/month**

Fee waived with $300 or more daily balance, a $25+ automatic recurring transfer, or a linked Chase Premier Plus Checking account. Given the $15,000 capital lockup required for the bonus, the fee waiver is automatically met. Net bonus after fees: $200 in all practical scenarios.

**Business Complete Checking: $15/month**

Fee waived with $2,000 or more daily balance (the same threshold as the bonus deposit requirement) or a linked Chase Ink business card. If the balance is maintained for the bonus, the fee is waived concurrently. Net bonus after fees: $300.

**Private Client: $0/month**

No monthly fee. Net bonus after fees: $1,000.

**Opportunity cost note for the $200 savings bonus:** $15,000 locked for 90 days in a 4.60% APY (Apr 2026) high-yield savings account would earn approximately $170. The $200 savings bonus outperforms by $30 before tax. The $900 combo math changes this calculation: the combined $900 bonus justifies the capital lockup more clearly.

- E*TRADE ACH push (2 confirmed reports -- highest evidence count)

- Fidelity NetBenefits employer payroll ACH (1 confirmed report)

- SoFi direct deposit pass-through (1 confirmed report)
- Capital One 360 ACH transfer (1 confirmed report)

**Does not qualify:**
- Zelle transfers (explicitly confirmed to fail -- Doctor of Credit, Dec 2025)

**Practical path:** If routing a payroll direct deposit through Chase for 1-2 pay periods is not viable, an ACH push from E*TRADE or Fidelity has the strongest direct deposit evidence. Initiate a transfer of $500 or more and wait for it to post with a "Direct Deposit" transaction descriptor before treating the requirement as met. Chase's 90-day window is generous enough to allow one retry if the first transfer posts as "ACH Transfer" rather than "Direct Deposit."

## Screening and Eligibility: Who Can Apply

**Chex/EWS sensitivity:** Chase uses Early Warning Services (EWS) only. Chase does not use ChexSystems. Negative ChexSystems entries from closures at other banks are not visible to Chase during the account opening review.

**Ineligible applicants:**
- Current Chase checking account holders
- Applicants who closed a Chase checking account within the past 90 days
- Applicants who closed a Chase account with a negative balance within the past 3 years
- Applicants who received a Chase checking bonus within the past 2 years (2-year cooldown applies per account type)

**Availability:** Nationwide. Chase Total Checking, Savings, and Business Complete Checking can be opened online or in-branch. Chase Private Client requires in-branch opening with a dedicated banker.

**No hard credit inquiry:** Standard Chase deposit account applications pull EWS only -- not a credit bureau inquiry. The application does not appear on credit reports.

## The Bottom Line

The Chase Total Checking offer nets $300 clean when the qualifying direct deposit waives the monthly fee -- a 120% effective bonus APR on $500 in capital over a 6-month holding period. No competing checking bonus in BonusClerk's database at a comparable capital lockup achieves a higher effective bonus APR.

**Open this account if:**
- Routing one paycheck or a $500+ ACH push from a brokerage (Fidelity, E*TRADE, Schwab) through Chase is feasible
- The $900 combo is the goal: pair Total Checking with Chase Savings and deposit $15,000 in new money for 90 days
- ChexSystems issues from a prior bank closure are a concern -- Chase's EWS-only Chex/EWS sensitivity means those marks are not visible

**Skip this account if:**
- A Chase checking bonus was received within the past 2 years (cooldown applies)
- A Chase checking account is currently open
- No fee waiver condition can be met and $228 net bonus after fees does not justify the effort
