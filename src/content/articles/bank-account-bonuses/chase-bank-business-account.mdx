## Requirement Stack

Three requirements, completed in sequence:

**1. Deposit new money.** $2,000 for the $300 tier or $10,000 for the $500 tier. Funds must be new to Chase -- transfers from existing Chase accounts or affiliates do not qualify. Deposit within 30 days of account opening.

**2. Maintain the balance for 60 days.** The full deposit amount must remain in the account for 60 calendar days. Dropping below $10,000 during this window may reduce the $500 bonus to $300.

**3. Complete 5 qualifying transactions within 90 days.** These are not direct deposits. Qualifying transaction types are covered in the next section.

**Ineligibility rules:**
- Currently hold a Chase business checking account
- Closed a Chase business checking account in the past 90 days
- Had a negative-balance closure at Chase in the past 3 years
- Received a Chase business checking bonus in the past 2 years

**EWS screening:** Chase co-owns Early Warning Services. The application triggers an EWS inquiry, not a ChexSystems pull. EWS records cannot be frozen.

## Qualifying Transactions: What Counts (and What Doesn't)

Chase Business Complete Checking does not require direct deposit. Instead, the bonus requires 5 qualifying transactions within 90 days of account opening. This is a meaningful differentiator from most bank bonuses.

**What counts as a qualifying transaction:**
- Debit card purchases (any amount, including $1)
- Chase Online Bill Pay payments
- ACH credits (inbound transfers from external accounts)
- Wire transfers (inbound or outbound)
- Chase QuickAccept deposits
- Mobile check deposits

**What does not count:**
- Zelle transfers
- ACH debits (outbound)
- P2P payments
- Online transfers to Chase credit cards

The simplest approach: make 5 small debit card purchases. Community data points confirm that transactions as low as $1 each satisfy the requirement. Five purchases at a gas station, grocery store, or coffee shop within the first week clears the transaction requirement entirely.

## Fee Math

The $15/mo maintenance fee on Business Complete Checking is waived when the account maintains a $2,000 daily balance. Since the minimum deposit for the $300 tier is $2,000 (and $10,000 for the $500 tier), the fee waiver is built into the requirement stack.

**$300 tier:** $300 bonus - $0 fees = $300 net bonus after fees

**$500 tier:** $500 bonus - $0 fees = $500 net bonus after fees

If the balance drops below $2,000 at any point during the 6-month keep-open window, the fee kicks in at $15/mo. Worst case over 6 months: $15/mo x 6 months = $90 total fee drag. Maintaining the minimum deposit avoids this entirely.

**Tax note:** Chase reports the bonus on a 1099-INT. This is interest income, not self-employment income. No SE tax is owed on the bonus.
